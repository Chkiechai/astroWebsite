# Introduction

Caring for your hair, eyebrows, and lashes is probably one of the most treacherous things to research out there--not including skincare. But we're not here to talk about skincare! That's in a different article. Today's topic is hair care, or more accurately, myth-busting and truth-telling.

Before we continue, though, I would like to point out that I am most definitely *not* a professional, and you should not take my words as the truth. However, I have based all of the things to be discussed off of true experiences I had trying all these 'secret hacks,' and what I say here should be fairly accurate. But, different people can tolerate different things, so it's up to you to know your hair.

# Common Myths & Misconceptions

So, there are a LOT of myths out there, and influencers are more than likely to either wholeheartedly believe them or lie to you about them. And you might be thinking, "Well, what's to stop this author from lying to me?" and if you are, kudos to you! I can't exactly make you believe that I'm telling the truth, but I promise that I am to the best of my abilities. Definitely fact-check me, though.

## Myths About Hair

1. **Hot Oil Treatments**:

Okay, so for some hair types, they do work. Dry, frizzy, or damaged hair definitely benefits from this. But here's the catch--you have to be careful not to make the oil too hot, and if you leave it in and don't wash it out, it's going to make your hair feel heavy. If you follow the process, you might see results, or you might not. Just my personal opinion here, but I just stick to normal hair oil that I make myself (I'll give the recipe later in the article).

2. **Food "Treatments"**:

I'm sure we've all seen these--mash an avocado up, add honey, maybe oil, and smear it through your hair? First of all, ew. Rubbing food through my hair does *not* sound nice to me. And while it *can* work, it's definitely not cheap! 

I mean, you probably see all of these hair care videos where they say "$0 Hair Growth In A Week" or something like that, but please consider that you've already bought those avocados. You spent money on them, intending to nourish your body with them, and now you're rubbing them through your hair. Just my opinion, but I think I'll stick with good old conditioner--maybe spend even a little extra on a higher-quality one!

Also, I would like to point out that a good product designed for hair will very likely work better than a food product that is meant for eating. Again, just my opinion, but there are a lot of fads out there, so be careful and do your own research.

3. **Castor Oil & Eyelashes**:

This one actually got me. When I first read about it, I was like, "Oh, I can get even longer, thicker lashes? Sign me up!" So, I got a bottle of castor oil, and followed all those tutorials for putting it above my lashes, on my lashes--everything. For a hot minute, I even thought it was working!

And then, my lashes broke and started falling out. I was *devastated*, because my lashes are naturally fairly long and thick, and in less than three days, I had harmed them because of my belief in an influencer.

Now, that's not to say that castor oil doesn't work--for people with dry, damaged lashes, it likely will! It rehydrates and nourishes, so they'll think that their lashes are growing because they're longer and less damaged. But, if you're just a normal person with normal lashes, please know that castor oil has not been proven to help with growth. In fact, it can clog the follicles where your lashes grow and actually thin them out, like what happened to me.

And girl, your lashes are already beautiful. Please don't hurt them!

Okay, so there are a lot of myths out there about hair treatments, and I cannot stress enough that you should do your own research, because I won't be covering all of them here. So, without further ado, let's move on to the next section--what works (at least for me, take this with a grain of salt). Hint: As always with my content, I hold the opinion that simple is better than complex and expensive!

# Hair Care--Simple = Better

So, for this, I figured I'd go over a full hair care routine, including the shower, because a lot of hair care also happens while showering! With that said, here's a step-by-step process to making sure your hair is in good health.

**In The Shower**:
1. Make sure the water is warm, but not too hot
2. Get hair completely wet
3. Massage shampoo onto scalp with fingertips (Scalp only)
4. Rinse
5. Repeat (Double shampooing is effective, because the first wash gets the top level of grease and grime, and then the second cleansing cleans your actual scalp)
6. Use conditioner on your ends, and let sit for 5-15 minutes (I recommend shaving and such during the wait)
7. Rinse with cool water

**Right After The Shower**:
1. Use your hands to gently squeeze excess water from your hair (Don't use a towel yet)
2. Apply leave-in conditioner (how much you use depends on your hair's thickness)
3. Carefully brush out tangles with a wet brush or large-toothed comb
4. Apply hair oil to the ends of your hair (Anywhere from 3-4 drops to a quarter-sized amount, depending on your hair's thickness & your scalp's sensitivity to weight)
5. Wrap in a microfiber towel

*NOTE: I said microfiber towel, because normal cotton towels can cause breakage, which is something we don't want. They're fairly inexpensive as well, if you  find the right brand, so that's a plus!*

**For Dry Hair (Non-Shower Days Only)**:
*Every Day:*
- Apply rosemary water to your scalp (a spray bottle, pump bottle, or by hand is fine, just make sure to gently massage it in)

*Every Other Day*:
- Apply hair oil to your ends (I recommend doing this at night, since it's kind of relaxing)

And that's my hair care routine! Which brings us to the next topic--recipes.

# Recipes

Now, if you really want to, you can buy your own hair oil and rosemary water. *But*, you've gotta watch out, because if you have a sensitive scalp or hair, those products can have ingredients that irritate them. As a fellow sensitive skinned person, I prefer to make my own hair oil and rosemary concoction, so I figured I'd share my recipes.

## Rosemary Water

*NOTE:* If you are using a microwave for this process, *please* be careful. Water can *superheat* in a microwave, causing it to violently erupt from its container when disturbed. Microwave the water in short bursts only, and make sure to wear insulated gloves when handling the jar.

**You Need:**
1. Dried Rosemary, 1 TSP
2. Clean Water, 1/2 Cup
3. Pot or jar (for heating)
4. Container, such as spray bottle or oil dropper (for holding the liquid when done)
5. Strainer / coffee filter
6. Small Spoon

**Directions:**

1. Add the *rosemary* to the *water*, and heat it (stove if using a pan, microwave* if using a jar (See above note))
2. Simmer the mixture for 8-10 minutes (If you're using the microwave, get the water hot, then stir for 2 minutes, then microwave for 30 seconds and repeat)
3. When it's finished, the water should be a light-deep golden color
4. Use the filter to strain out the rosemary leaves, let water cool, and pour into your desired container.
5. **IMPORTANT:** If the water is more than 1 month old, it has turned a deep reddish-pink, or it has little red-black floating particles in it, toss it out and make a new batch.

## Homemade Hair Oil

1. 2 TBSP Olive oil (Hydration & nourishment)
2. 3 TBSP Grapeseed oil *or* coconut oil (shine & health)
3. Container of choice (Oil dropper or empty and washed baby oil container work perfectly)

**Directions**:

1. Mix oils together
2. Put in desired container
3. Replace every 1.5-2 months
