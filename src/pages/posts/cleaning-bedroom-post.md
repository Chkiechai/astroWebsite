# Introduction

Now, this post isn't about how to *organize* your bedroom--that's a decision that is entirely up to the person working on it. Everyone has different tastes when it comes to organization. However, in order to organize, you first have to actually *clean* your bedroom (ugh), so here's the breakdown of my methods, and how I can deep clean my room in only 1-2 hours.

# How To Clean Your Bedroom

## Step 1: Sectioning

So, first of all, we can't just go off half-cocked on this--we need a system or we're going to end up sporadically cleaning parts of our bedrooms for weeks (I've done this before). So, a method I like to use (and I doubt that I'm the one who made it up, I'm just sharing my thoughts) is sectioning. I divide my room into 5 parts, based on how messy it is and such.

1. **Bed Space**: This is my bed, nightstand, etc.
2. **Desk Space**: My desk, under my desk, and the chair
3. **Closet Space**: This section is the entirety of my closet, so the shelves, clothing, shoes, etc
4. **Other Space**: This part can contain whatever last part of your room that wasn't listed here--in my case, it's my dresser and makeup shelf
5. **Floor Space**: This is just the floor area, and everything on the floor that doesn't belong, such as laundry

## Step 2: Cleaning

I'm going to go through each section of the above list in order, but you don't have to clean in the order they are portrayed! Feel free to start from anywhere on the list, because as long as it all gets done, there's no need to go in order. If you don't have one of the sections, or something inside the section, don't worry! You can just skip it!

### Bed Space

Alright, we're doing a deep clean here, so here are the steps to an effective one:

1. **The Bed**:
   - Change the sheets and replace with clean ones
   - Make your bed
   - Take a broom/vacuum cleaner and clean out underneath it (if there's stuff under there, pull it out beforehand)
2. **The Nightstand**:
   - Take *everything* off / out of it
   - Wipe it down
   - Put the things back in there, but organized in the way you like (*Tip: Only keep the essentials on top of your nightstand, such as a light, speaker, etc. The rest can go inside it or somewhere else in your room*)

Alright, the bed space is done! Now, we can move on to desk space!

### Desk Space

Here's a step-by-step design for cleaning your desk off / out!

1. **The Desk**:
   - Clean everything out/off of the desk
   - Dust the surface, and wipe it down
   - Clean the items that you cleared off (dust things like a laptop, pencil holder, light, etc)
   - Refill/organize your desk (only put the essentials on the top of the desk)
2. **Under The Desk**:
   - If there's stuff under there, sweep it out!
   - Vacuum / sweep beneath desk
   - Dust (if needed)
3. **The Chair**:
   - Vacuum chair (seat fabric, etc)
   - Dust all hard surfaces (arm rests, legs, etc)
   - Wipe down the chair with damp rag (sometimes it gets oily because of skin contact)

And now the desk is complete! Time to start on the closet.

### Closet Space

This part tends to take the longest out of all of it, but when it gets finished it's *so* nice! So, without further ado, let's get cleaning!

1. **Clothing**:
   - Take every single item of clothing out of your closet (I like to put them on my bed so they stay clean)
   - *(Optional) Use this time to get rid of clothes that don't fit, you don't like, or never wear*
   - If you have empty hangers, put them in one side of your closet for easy access
   - Put the clothing back into your closet, but this time in a more organized way (*For reference, I like to sort my clothing like this (left to right): Athletic wear, tank tops, t-shirts, long sleeves, hoodies, jackets, sweaters*)
2. **Shoes**:
   - Remove shoes from closet
   - *(Optional) Purge shoes that you don't like or never wear*
   - Dust shoes / clean shoe area
   - Put shoes back
3. **Shelves**:
   - Remove items from shelves
   - Dust and wipe down items
   - Dust and wipe down shelves
   - Put items back on the shelves
   - *NOTE: If you have spare bedsheets on your closet shelves, and they're not folded well, refold them--trust me, it's nice!*

Now, the closet is done, and we can move on to the other space! This one doesn't really need a set of specific instructions, since it will be different for everyone. Here's a basic plan, though, that works for nearly everything:

1. Dust surfaces / items
2. *(Optional) Purge unwanted things*
3. Organize & Prioritize open spaces

And last but not least, we move on to cleaning the floor space.

### Floor Space

This is by far the fastest of all the spaces! The steps go something like this:

1. Pick up laundry and items that don't belong there (toys, shoes, school / work stuff, etc)
2. Sweep and lightly mop (with a damp rag or one of those swiffer things)
3. Vacuum any rugs / carpeting

And we're done!

# Closing

Hopefully this article gave you an overview of how to properly deep clean your bedroom--there are certainly things I missed, and everyone's room is different, but as long as you follow a basic cleaning procedure like the one for the other space section, your room will be clean! And if you make a habit of doing some super basic cleanup every morning or evening, such as laundry and dusting, your room will always be clean.

I hope this helped!
