# Introduction

The living room is a very important part of a house or apartment. It's where you host guests, watch TV, or just hang out in general--which means it's going to be infested with dishes and dust almost immediately. So, in this article, I will be discussing tactics on *how* to deep clean your living room (according to my personal methods), and then maintain it afterwards.

# How to Clean Your Living Room

As always with my articles, we're going to start by breaking down the living room into sections to make our job easier. Now, there will likely be things that I miss, because everyone's living room is different, and for those parts, you may put them in the 'other space.' And if I list a section you don't have, then no worries, you can just skip it!

## Step 1: Sectioning

Here's a breakdown of the general spaces in a living room:

1. **Sitting Space**: This includes chairs, couches, lamps, and coffee tables (plus the contents of the coffee tables)
2. **TV Space**: This is the TV, TV stand / shelf, speakers, game consoles, etc.
3. **Plant Space(?)**: This is an optional space, but a lot of people have plants in their living room. If you are one of those people, you will have this space!
4. **Other Space**: This includes things I didn't list above or didn't think to list
5. **Floor & Wall Space**: This is the floor, rug / carpeting, and walls (baseboards, window frames, ceiling lights, etc.) (This one comes last--otherwise you'll have to clean it twice)

## Step 2: Cleaning

I will be going down the list above for ease of reading, but don't feel constrained to that! As long as the floor and walls section comes last, you can mix up any of the other sections according to your preferences.

Below are the general essentials you'll need:

1. Vacuum cleaner (preferably with a hose attachment option, but that's not technically essential)
2. Broom & dustpan (if you don't have hard flooring, you don't need these)
3. Duster (Feather, foam, cloth, etc.)
4. Windex & Paper towels
5. Microfiber cloth (for the TV screen)

Now that we have the essentials, it's time to start cleaning!

### Sitting Space

Here's the general breakdown of how to clean this area:

1. **Chair/Couch**:
   - If it has removeable cushions, take them off, along with any pillows and blankets you might have on there
   - Vacuum the area that the cushions rest on top of, and then vacuum the cushions as well (this is where a hose attachment comes in handy)
   - Replace everything on the couch / chair, making sure to style the pillows nicely and fold any blankets
2. **Lamps**: (*Note*: *this includes floor and table lamps*)
   - Turn off the lamps (You'll be touching the base and bulbs with a damp rag, and better safe than sorry)
   - Using a damp rag, carefully wipe off any dust and grime from each lamp
   - Let them dry, then go over them again with the duster
   - *(Optional) Vacuum the lamp shades--it's really nice*
3. **Coffee Table(s)**:
   - *For wooden / stone table*: Wipe down with damp rag, then use duster
   - *For glass table*: Use a small amount of Windex and a paper towel or three. Dust if needed
   - Clean all items on coffee table with a damp rag *(unless it's paper or cloth, or uses electricity. For those, just use a duster)*

Alright! Time for the next section.

### TV Space

*NOTE: Never use Windex or normal cloth when cleaning a TV screen!! It can and will strip the anti-glare coating and damage the screen!*

Here's my process:

1. **The TV**:
   - Dust the frame of the TV and the back of it with a duster
   - Use a microfiber cloth and gently dust the screen. Gently, mind you!
   - Dust the stand / shelf as well
2. **Speakers**:
   - If you don't have external speakers, skip this part.
   - If you do, then dust them with a dry duster
3. **Game Consoles**:
   - Dust the game consoles
   - *(Optional) Organize your games / CDs / controllers*

Also, if you keep books on top of your speakers or TV stand or just in the space in general, dust them. With that said, let's move on to the plants section!

### Plant(s) Space

1. **The Plants**:
   - Water them (Make sure to research how much water and how often they need it!)
2. **The Pots / Containers**:
   - *Cloth / Basket weave*: Use a dry duster
   - *Clay / Plastic*: Use a damp rag
   - *Glass / Other non-porous*: Use Windex and paper towels

For the 'other space', everyone's living room is a little different, but if you follow a basic procedure (plus your adaptations) it should be clean like the rest of the living room.

Here's a general procedure:

1. Dust and/or wipe down
2. Vacuum (if needed)
3. Organize

And those two sections are done! Now, for the final bit of cleanup.

### Floor & Wall Space

1. **The Walls**:
   - Wipe down the baseboards with a damp rag
   - Wipe any dirt, dust, or grime off of main walls
   - Clean windows with Windex & paper towels
   - Dust ceiling for cobwebs and dust ceiling lights as well
2. **The Floor**:
   - *For carpeting*: Vacuum carpeting
   - *For rugs & hard flooring*: Vacuum rugs and sweep hard flooring. If it's tile or fake wood, mop as well
   - *For plain hard flooring*: Sweep floor, and if it's not real wood, mop and let air-dry

We're done!

# Closing

Now that the living room is clean, we want it to stay that way, right? So here's a general maintenance routine that I follow to make sure the room never gets too bad.

DAILY:
1. Sweep
2. Dust
3. Organize

ONCE PER WEEK:
1. Clean windows
2. Vacuum

ONCE PER 2 WEEKS:
1. Deep clean

With this schedule, I've found that my living room is fairly clean all the time, and I feel comfortable hosting guests in it. I hope this article helped!
