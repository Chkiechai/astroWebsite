# Introduction

Ugh. Bathrooms. That one room that you're told to clean, but were probably never taught *how*. Maybe you've figured out a pretty good method, or maybe you've never cleaned one before but now you have to because you just moved into your own place--either way, worry no more, because this article is going to go into depth on how to deep clean your bathroom, and then maintain it!

# How To Clean A Bathroom

So, as with every other article I've ever written about cleaning a place, we're going to start by dividing the bathroom into sections, because this is the most effective method I know of. Let's begin!

## Step 1: Sectioning

Pretty much all bathrooms are the same, but if there's something not in the list below, just know it belongs in the 'other' section. Here are the sections:

1. **Sink Space**: This is the sink, mirror, and everything on / in the sink's cabinets and drawers
2. **Toilet Space**: The toilet, toilet paper, trash can, etc.
3. **Tub Space**: The tub, curtain, and everything inside the tub (such as shampoo, razors, combs, etc)
4. **Towel Space**: All the towels and their respective hook / hangers
5. **Floor & Wall Space**: The floors, baseboards, and walls (where splatters happen, such as makeup, toothpaste, etc) (Save this for last)

## Step 2: Cleaning

I will be going down the list in order, but that doesn't mean you have to! Feel free to jump around the list, as long as the floor and wall space is the last one you clean (because if you clean them first, they'll get dirty again when you clean the other stuff). If there's a step for something that you don't have, no worries, just skip it!

Below are the basic cleaning essentials you *definitely* need for cleaning a bathroom:
- Anti-bacterial wipes (Clorox, etc)
- Glass cleaner (Windex)
- Bleach (Toilet bowl bleach, counter top bleach, or tub spray (They usually all work on the same things, just check the directions))
- A toilet brush
- One of those tough green scrubbers
- Rubber Gloves (Technically optional, but nice)
- Paper Towels
- Broom & Mop
- A large cup (That you will *absolutely never drink out of*)
- Trash bags (For a bathroom garbage bag, I usually use those thin plastic shopping bags from groceries)

Once you have the essentials, you can begin cleaning. (This is the part where you put on those rubber gloves, if you've got them!)

### Sink Space

Here's my go-to process!

1. **Sink**:
   - Use the anti-bacterial wipes and carefully wipe down the sink faucet and handles, making sure to get rid of any streaking, toothpaste, makeup, etc.
   - Spray a small amount of bleach in the sink bowl (make sure you're well-ventilated or wearing a mask) and use the green scrubber to clear off any grime
   - Use the cup (filled with water) to rinse the bleach off of the sink's bowl
2. **Counter Top & Cabinets**:
   - Clear *everything* off of the top of the sink (I just put my stuff on the floor or a different clean surface)
   - Use another anti-bacterial wipe (or several) on the counter, making sure to really clean the corners where it meets the wall (if it does)
   - Get everything out of the cabinets and drawers, and set it aside for now
   - Use an anti-bacterial wipe to dust and clean the inside of the cabinets and drawers and wait for them to dry
3. **The Stuff In / On The Counter & Cabinets**:
   - If it's a towel, rag, paper towel, toilet paper, or anything soft, put it back where it goes (this is a good time to organize)
   - Any hard items, such as shampoo bottles, baskets, etc. need to be cleaned--wipe them down with a damp rag
   - Replace them in their designated spots
4. **The Mirror**:
   - Spritz the mirror lightly with Windex, and use a paper towel or three to carefully wipe it off (avoid streaking where possible!)
   - Use an anti-bacterial wipe to clean the mirror's frame

And, the sink is done!

### Toilet Space

Now, cleaning a toilet is a nasty business, and it's definitely my least favorite part--but once we deep clean, maintaining that cleanliness is easy! Let's get to it.

1. **The Toilet Bowl**:
   - First, make sure the toilet has fresh water in it (flush if need be)
   - Lift both seats, then spray a light coating of bleach all around the inside of the bowl
   - Use the toilet brush to scrub the bowl
   - Once it's totally clean, flush the toilet
2. **The Toilet Exterior**:
   - Using the anti-bacterial wipes, wipe down both sides of both toilet seats and the lid
   - Wipe down the space behind the lid & the outside of the bowl
   - Clean the handle and the top of the toilet (including the outside of the tank (We'll leave the inside of the tank alone for now))
   - Wipe down the bottom of the toilet

The toilet is now finished (finally).

### Tub Space

The tub part of cleaning the bathroom takes the longest, because it requires a little more precision. (Same goes if you only have a shower, just adapt to your needs)

1. **The Tub**:
   - Remove everything from the tub, such as shampoo, conditioner, etc. and set it aside
   - Spray bleach all along the bottom of the tub, and scrub it *hard* with the green scrubber
   - Use the cup (filled with water) to rinse the bleach out
2. **The Shelves**:
   - Spritz bleach on any shelves you might have and scrub them clean
   - Rinse them off (Carefully--you don't want watery bleach splattered all over your clothes and skin!)
3. **The Walls**:
   - If you have flat walls, just lightly spray them and scrub them
   - If you have tiled walls, make sure to clean *every* crack between the tiles
   - Rinse the walls with water when finished
4. **The Items**:
   - Using a damp rag or anti-bacterial wipes, clean all bottles that go in the tub
   - Use water to rinse out loofahs, soap containers, etc.
   - Put items back where they belong

For the towel space, it takes such a small amount of time, I won't give it its own header. Here's what you need to know:

1. Throw current towels in laundry
2. Wipe off the towel rack / hooks
3. Replace with fresh towels

With that done, let's move on to the final step in our process, the floor & walls space.

### Floor & Walls Space

1. **The Floor**:
   - If you have bath rugs, beat the dirt off of them (OUTSIDE) and wash if needed
   - Sweep & mop
2. **The Walls**:
    - Clean any splatters
    - Wipe down baseboards with anti-bacterial wipes
    - Clean light switches* with anti-bacterial wipes

* ***PLEASE NOTE:*** I do not know what your furnishings are! If you have light switches and/or outlets that are not covered, revealing bare wires or metal, DO NOT, and I mean ABSOLUTELY DO NOT touch them with a wipe! Do not even clean them with anything even remotely wet! 

# Closing

Now, your bathroom is beautiful, shiny, and clean! To maintain it, I recommend a deep clean once every 2-3 weeks, and every week, do a basic cleaning:

1. Wipe toilet, sink, and cabinets (And scrub toilet)
2. Replace toilet paper
3. Change towels
4. Scrub tub with scrubber and water and/or bleach
5. Sweep & beat rugs

I hope this article helped!
