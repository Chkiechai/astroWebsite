# Introduction

Buying new things can be so much fun! We can redecorate, change up our clothes, and get delicious food--but it gets to be fairly expensive after a while. However, even though everything is expensive, we still yearn for new things, because it's exciting to think about all the possibilities. So, in this article, I'm going to share my views on what I call wishlist window (WW) shopping, although I do my shopping online, so there's no actual windows.

# What Is WW Shopping?

The idea behind wishlist window shopping is simple: whenever you find something you like and want, put it on a list. If it's something that's *truly* necessary, like food for the week, it can be on a necessities list. But, if it's just a want, no matter how much you desire it, put it on the wishlist. There's no limit to how large a wishlist can be--the point is just to have it all saved somewhere.

Once the wishlist is made, it's time to check with yourself--can the item you want be afforded right now? If it can, should it even be bought?

For this concept, the answer is *no*. Even if you can afford it, don't buy it yet. Keep it on the list, and pretend that you can't afford it yet. A few weeks or months later, when the initial want has cooled a little, go back over the wishlist, and then decide whether or not to buy the item.

This is wishlist window shopping. It doesn't *seem* like it'd be fun, does it? Pretending to be unable to afford the item, keeping it on a list for months before deciding to buy it--it seems like a waste of time, and what if the item is gone by the time you come back? But I promise you, dear reader, it *can* be fun.

# Is WW Shopping Actually Fun?

If it's done correctly, WW shopping can be better than normal shopping. Why? Because there's something so whimsical about pretending you can't afford something! In a world where everyone wants instant gratification, actually *waiting* can be more fun, because here's the thing that I've discovered (at least about myself), the anticipation of getting something is far more fun than owning the thing.

Imagine this: you're in a store, looking at, say, a new line of your favorite makeup. You find one you really like and buy it immediately. It *is* fun, there's no denying it. But there's also no sense of accomplishment--no feeling of triumph, because you didn't have to wait. It's just another thing you bought, and even though you might be excited, it's still nothing really special.

Now imagine you tried WW Shopping with this same item. You find the product you want and check the price tag, and see that it's around $5. Now, instead of putting it in your cart, you wince, shake your head like that item is way too expensive, and put it back on the shelf. You'll wait until later, even though you *really* want the product. A few weeks later, you come back to the store, and if they still have the product (and you still really want it), you buy it. Now, that sense of anticipation and excitement is much stronger, because you waited! You imagined what it would be like to have that item, and now you *finally* have it!

It sounds kind of weird, but it's made my shopping experiences far more exciting and fulfilling--and it also works with online shopping! Just put the desired item in your online cart, and wait for a few weeks. When and if you finally buy it, it's probably going to feel like more of an accomplishment.

And that's the joy of wishlist window shopping in a nutshell!

# Closing

I hope this article was helpful, and if not helpful, then interesting! Wishlist window shopping might not be for everyone, but for those out there who think it could be fun--it totally is! I encourage you to give it a shot, and feel free to make your own adaptations--all I shared here was a basic outline, not a hard rule set.

Enjoy your shopping spree!
