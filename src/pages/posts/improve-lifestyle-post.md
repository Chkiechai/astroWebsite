# Introduction

This article isn't meant to be some kind of influencer "look at me *I'm* thriving on a (supposed) budget" post. To have a good lifestyle, you don't need to have a million products, aesthetics, or fancy foods. I know *I* can't afford that, and yet I feel pretty good about my current lifestyle--I have a clean place, I'm working hard and have good grades, and I feel pretty good just in general.

The root of the issue is not the things you buy, the prices, or the aesthetics--it's the environment and the mindset.

# The Mindset

This part will take a little more time. First off, you should know that the mindset part of this article is not about telling yourself over and over that you'll do better. It's about actually improving your mood using tried and true methods!

## Movement

That's right! I said *movement,* not *exercise*. You don't need a rigorous, three-hour exercise program to improve your life, but a little bit of movement every day really does do wonders. This can be things like yoga, walking, dancing to music as you make breakfast--whatever you like, as long as you're moving your whole body!

I know personally, I like a short, 5->10 minute exercise session every day. Something kind of like this:
- 15 Bicycle crunches
- 15 Arm circles
- 15 Leg raises

This way, I'm working my legs, arms, and core, and I never even have to break a sweat.

## Self Care

Now, when I say self care, it probably brings to mind heavy makeup, expensive facial products, and extravagant clothing. That is *not* what I'm talking about when I say self care! It shouldn't be about *what* products you buy or use, but how you use the ones you already have. It's about your diet (not saying to cut out sweets, but maybe lessen them a little), and your clothing.

When you feel clean, healthy, and confident in your outfit, your mood will improve. 

I know in terms of my self care, I tend to stick to a *very* simple routine.

1. **Hygiene**--I wash my face, put on deodorant, apply light makeup, etc.
2. **Diet**--I usually try to have two healthy meals a day, and the third is based on what I'm craving
3. **Clothing**--I tend to like simpler clothing, so wide-leg jeans and a shirt and cardigan is my go-to

Simple as that! No fancy accessories or products, just a routine that makes me feel awake and confident!

## Money

I was going to put this in the environment section, but after some thought, I decided it fit more in the mindset part. Why? Because the reasons as to *why* you spend your money is up to you, not your environment.

Here's the basic concept--obviously, you still have to spend money on things, and sometimes you *really*, *really* want that random thing at Target! (I've been there, trust me) But, that doesn't change the fact that money is not infinite, and gas and groceries are *expensive*.

Everyone's financial situation is different, so I can't really give a lot of input into this, but here's a basic plan that is flexible and works pretty well for me:

- When I get my paycheck, 60-80% of it goes into savings
- With the other 20-40%, I take care of my groceries/gas/other life expenses
- If I have money left over, I allow myself a splurge buy (under $30-50 in my case, but it differs for everyone)

So, the concept behind a splurge buy is this: once per month, I let myself buy something that I don't necessarily *need,* but really want. And to keep myself from having regrets after buying it, I'll usually build myself a wishlist, and let it sit until I feel like splurge buying. Then, if I still *really* want whatever it is, I'll let myself buy it!

# Closing

Overall, even if you don't follow my specific lifestyle (which is totally fine, because it's *your* life,) I hope this helped you see things in a different perspective, and I encourage you to find your favorite way of living, and *live it*.
