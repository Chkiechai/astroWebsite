# Introduction

What's the worst thing to find when you're in the mood to cook? A messy kitchen with dishes everywhere! (Although, it's bad no matter what culinary mood you're in). You can't have a clear mind and good mood when you walk into your kitchen and it's a disaster (trust me, I've been there)--so, here's my method to deep clean my kitchen in only a few hours (depending on the size of kitchen and the things in it), and the system I've built to never let it become a disaster again.

# How To Clean Your Kitchen

## Step 1: Section It!

Now, this is quite a lot like my article about cleaning your bedroom, with some (rather big) differences. But, as with any cleaning method, it's important to have a plan, or the cleaning will be sporadic and not that effective. To clean a kitchen, I've found that dividing it into 6 sections helps to keep my brain organized.

Also note, when I say hot water, I *do not* mean boiling water. I mean properly warm water, probably around 100-110° Fahrenheit.

1. **Cooking Space**: This is your stove, cooking utensils (spatula, knives, cutting boards, etc), and oven.
2. **Food Space**: Consists of the pantry, fridge, freezer, etc.
3. **Cookware Space**: This has the pans, coffee/tea brewing equipment, eating dishes, silverware, etc.
4. **Other Space**: Everyone's kitchen is different, so if there's something in yours that I didn't list, it goes here
5. **Sink Space**: This part is the sink and dishwashing equipment (towels, rags, soap storage, etc.)
6. **Counter & Floor Space**: The counter area and floor

## Step 2: Cleaning

Now, we're moving on to the cleaning part of this, which will take the longest. I'm going to go through each space from the list above in order, but that isn't a strict requirement. Except for the last two in the list: Sink Space and Counter & Floor space. Those should come last, in that order, since if you do them before the rest of the sections they're probably going to get dirty again.

For each space, I will be giving a step-by-step on how to really deep clean it.

### Cooking Space

1. **The Stove**:
   - If the stove has grates above the burners, remove them and set them in the sink for now
   - Wipe down the stove surface (For this part, I use a combination of baking soda and dish soap, for abrasiveness and grease destruction) (*Make sure to wipe the excess baking soda and soap off of the stove when you're done if you're using this method*)
   - Use a damp rag to clean the stove knobs (They get *so* greasy)
   - Clean the grates (if you have them) by using hot water, soap, and a tough scrubber such as a steel wire scrubber or one of those green ones, then dry them and put them back on the stove
2. **The Oven**:
   - Remove oven racks and wash them with soap and hot water
   - Clean interior of oven with damp rag (Make sure to remove any piece of burnt food!)
   - Use Windex on the exterior of the glass on the oven door (Wipe it off with a paper towel)
   - Clean the interior glass with a vinegar-water solution, or just plain water
   - Replace oven racks
   - Wipe down outside of oven with damp rag (If you have an oven with metal handles or design, I recommend using a bit of Windex on that instead of water, to avoid streaking)
3. **Cooking Utensils**:
   - Take all spatulas, cooking spoons, etc. out of their containers, and put them by the sink (this includes the knife caddy or however you store your knives)
   - Wash the utensil containers with soap and hot water
   - Dry the containers and load the utensils back into them (wash any dirty/dusty utensils)
   - Put the now full containers back where they were (or reorganize, it's up to you)
   - Wash any other cooking utensils (excluding things from the cookware section)

Alright, the cooking space is done! Now, let's move on to the next section.

### Food Space

1. **Pantry**:
   - Remove everything from shelves / drawers
   - Clean the shelves / drawers with a damp rag and if necessary, a vacuum cleaner or duster
   - Wipe down each food item as you replace it in the pantry, and get rid of anything expired
2. **Fridge**:
   - Remove everything from your fridge, and put it in a cooler
   - Wipe down the shelves, drawers, and walls of the fridge with a damp rag
   - Replace food items, and get rid of expired or gross-tasting foods
   - Wipe down exterior of fridge using Windex or a damp rag
3. **Freezer**:
   - Use the same method as for the fridge, but be careful not to let the freezer defrost. I recommend doing a shelf at a time, over the course of a few hours / days. (Don't leave the door open for very long, in other words)

Now, the food area is clean, so we can start on the cookware section.

### Cookware Space

1. **Pans & Pots**:
   - Wash unclean pots and pans, and wipe down the others with a fresh damp rag
   - Wipe down any shelves, hooks, or drawers where the pots and pans were resting
   - Put the now clean cookware back
2. **Coffee / Tea Brewing Equipment**:
    - Clean *everything*. Wipe it down, wash what you can with hot water and soap, dry it, and dust it
    - Make sure to clean underneath any machines, if you have them (such as espresso machines, water kettles, etc)
    - *(Optional) Organize your coffee or tea flavors*
3. **Eating Dishes & Silverware**:
   - Wash any dirty plates, bowls, and silverware
   - Pull all eating equipment out of its storage space and wipe down shelves / drawers with damp rag
   - Put everything back

And--the cookware area is finished!! Now, for the other space, just follow the general cleaning list:
1. Unload stuff
2. Wipe it down
3. Load it back
4. Organize (Optional)

Once that's done, we can move on to the sink space.

### Sink Space

1. **The Sink**:
   - If you have a food catcher in your drain(s), empty it out in a trash can
   - If you have a two-sided sink, one for washing and one with a drying rack, remove the drying rack and set it aside
   - Mix baking soda and coarse salt together--it should be a ratio of Soda : Salt = 1 : 2--and use a scrubber or brush to clean the inside of your sink with the mixture (baking soda and salt are a classic abrasive mixture, good for getting stains out. You can also add a bit of dish soap for an extra oomph)
   - Rinse out the mix and put food catchers (If you've got them) back into the drains
   - Wash the dish rack (If you have one) and put it back as well
2. **Rags & Towels**:
   - Toss any dirty towels or rags into the laundry to be washed
3. **Soap Storage**:
   - Wipe down your soap bottles with a damp rag (hand soap, dish soap, etc)
   - Dry them and put them back

Now that the sink is nice and clean, let's move on to the floor and counter space!

### Floor & Counter Space

1. **Floor**:
    - Pick up anything that doesn't belong
    - Sweep & Mop
    - Wipe down the walls as well (Baseboards, and any areas where food can get splattered, such as by a stove, sink, etc)
    - Dust light fixtures too
2. **Counters**:
   - Organize anything that doesn't belong, give everything a place
   - Wipe down with damp rag and dry surface

And we're done! You've now deep cleaned your entire kitchen!

# Closing

There you are! An easy, step-by-step process for cleaning your kitchen. Once I've deep cleaned my kitchen, I know I don't want it to get really bad again, so I try to stick to a schedule kind of like this:

**Mon-Fri**:
1. Wash dishes
2. Dust
3. Sweep
4. Wipe down surfaces (counters, walls, stove top)

**Sat**:
1. Do all weekday stuff
2. Mop
3. Clean interior of oven (And clean glass too, if I feel like it)
4. Wipe down fridge
5. Wipe down shelving, drawers, etc

With this, my kitchen stays clean all the time--even when I cook big, messy meals. I hope this helped!
