# Introduction

Believe it or not, the secret to taking good care of your skin is *not* having expensive, exotic products. Or smearing food on your face and calling it a mask. Those things are actually harmful, in most cases, because your skin is good at taking care of itself, and it only needs a little bit of help.

So, before I get started on good products that don't break the bank, let's debunk some 'skincare tips.' Also, there are a lot of myths out there, and I *will not* be covering all of them because that's probably impossible, so before you use a skincare product, I advise doing some research, and make sure you know your skin's sensitivity.

# Common Myths & Misconceptions

**Myth 1**: *Expensive products are always better.*
- They're not. Really, I promise. Also, if the product has so many ingredients you can't even read them all, or the ingredients are unpronounceable, that *does not necessarily make it a good product*.

A good example of this is Niacinamide, which is, incidentally, my second 'tip.'

**Myth 2**: *Niacinamide is a cure-all for pore reduction, skin smoothing, etc.*
- Okay, so for some people, it is really good for them! It does reduce pores, inflammation, etc. But what they don't tell you is *how* they get it to work. Niacinamide is pretty reactive, and if, per chance, you use a facial cleanser that contains it, just the temperature of your water, the light, or combination with vitamin C can cause it to turn (time varies, sometimes minutes, sometimes days) into Niacin.
- Niacin, for a lot of people who normally don't have trouble with Niacinamide, can cause irritation and redness.

Also, I know it's not like this for everyone, but I myself am *very* allergic to Niacinamide. When I use products containing it, I break out in itchy, red hives that take a long time to fade. So, if you *do* use Niacinamide products, spot test first, and be careful.

**Myth 3**: *You need a lot of different types of moisturizer for your face and skin, or it will become unhealthy.*
  - No. No, you definitely do not. Again, your skin is *pretty good* at taking care of itself, so slathering a hundred different creams onto it is more likely to inflame it than make it smoother. Plus, it'll make it feel more greasy and maybe even unclean, trust me.

These are three common myths that I see everywhere. Again, I won't be going over all skincare myths in this article, so I encourage you to do a lot of research when considering trying something new. Now, let's move on to the actual skincare routine (hint: it's pretty simple!).

# A Simple, Effective Routine

So, there's a variety of different skin types, and there's not a hard line between all of them--you can have oily *and* normal skin, or normal skin, or dry and oily skin, etc. With that said, I won't give you advice on how many times a day to wash your face, except please don't do it more than twice, because that's really not good for your skin.

That aside, let's get into the actual process that I use. (Feel free to adapt, this is just an example of what works for me to use as a reference if you like)

**MORNINGS**:
 For this, I usually just wash my face with plain, ice cold water. This is because it wakes me up and helps me focus. Unless I skipped washing my face the night before or my skin is especially oily that day, I don't use a cleanser.

**EVENINGS**:
This is where most of my skincare routine comes in, because it's the time when I wash off the day's makeup. Here's a list of my steps:
1. *Washing my face*:
      - Wet face with warm water
      - Double cleanse--once for getting rid of makeup, once for cleansing the dirtier skin oil (I use a gel cleanser)
2. *Moisturizer*:
   - I use a simple, cheap lotion made for sensitive skin, and I only use a small amount of it--enough to cover my face but not enough to feel thick and greasy

And that's it! If I am in the middle of a breakout, I might use a pimple patch or two (put those on before lotion), but other than that, I don't do much fancy stuff. With that done, let's move on to the products that I have acd use.

# Products

So, the fun thing about buying products is it's really hard to tell which products are good, especially in my case because I might use one brand's product, then be allergic to a different product from the same company. So, here's some simple, relatively inexpensive products I use:

1. Face wash--*Vanicream Gentle Facial Cleanser* or *Beauty of Joseon Green Plum Refreshing Gel Cleanser* (A mouthful of a name, am I right?)
2. Moisturizer--*Vanicream Daily Facial Moisturizer (Sensitive Skin)*
3. Extra--If my lips are cracked from winter weather or something like that, I'll use a bit of *Aquaphor Healing Ointment* on them

# Closing

So, there you go! A simple skincare routine is always better for maintaining your skin's natural glow and health. I hope this article was helpful!
