# Introduction

Now, before we get started, I'm not going to promise that this will truly help you study more efficiently. *You* are in charge of making sure that you find the right technique for *you*. However, I figured I'd share some of my tools for fooling my brain into actually wanting to study, do homework, and *yes*, stop procrastinating!

## The Now Habit

* I should point out that this part of the article references a book written by Neil Fiore. I DID NOT write *The Now Habit*, but I found it incredibly useful, so I definitely recommend that you give it a read!

*The Now Habit* is a great way to study; it focuses not on buckling down and torturing yourself into studying, but on addressing the core causes of *why* exactly you were procrastinating in the first place. (Hint, it's not necessarily laziness) Maybe you're tired, or you feel like you won't do a good job now, but might do better tomorrow.

I've been there.

Here's my take on it--stop thinking about it so negatively. Instead of saying to yourself "I won't do good on it right now," say "I'm going to do this now, and I will do my best. And tomorrow, I will look it over, and I will reinforce it even more."

This, believe it or not, can change your mindset on this, a little bit at a time.

And if you're just feeling lazy, and you don't want to do the work right now, then don't! At least--not *all* of it. Try to do just a little. Write one sentence on that English homework. Do one math problem. Then, maybe do another. Before you know it, you'll have completed the assignment, your mood will have improved because now you don't have that deadline over your head, and you can get back to that show you were binge watching!

Now that we've discussed this part of studying, let's talk about *when* to study, and how to reframe it in your mind so it actually makes you look forward to it!

## Studying Time

Here's a breakdown of my daily schedule (prepare yourself):

- 4:40 a.m. -> 5:00 a.m: Wake up, get ready for the day (I'm a morning person)
- 5:00 a.m. -> 6:00 a.m: Free hour
- 6:01 a.m. -> 8:00 a.m: Homework
- 8:30 a.m. -> 3:00 p.m: School
- 3:00 p.m. -> 5:00 p.m: Homework

INSANE. Crazy.

Now, I know my particular schedule is not for everyone. But as a homeschooled student who is trying to teach herself both pre-calculus AND nuclear physics on top of all her other classes, my particular schedule is warranted. However, I encourage you to look past the times on the list above, and see the routine: *wake up, free time, study, school, study, free time*.

Also, when I say *study*, I don't mean sitting in a dark room, eyes burning with exhaustion, brain sluggish and mood crashing as I slowly realize I didn't do last week's homework. I mean *movie-style* studying. The kind of studying where you're sitting in your room, or a cafe, or even the library, listening to music, sipping coffee (or tea). The kind of work that lets you take nice notes, take breaks, and really *learn*. 

It's not too hard, really. It might take a few weeks, but if you keep up with a schedule like this, soon, you might be so ahead in school, you might not even have to *do* homework some days!

**Romanticize it. Pretend you're a mysterious, overachieving character in a movie.**

So wake up a little earlier. Make your bed, and get dressed. Get a bit of food, and a coffee or tea, and study as much as you can before school. Eventually, it will be a habit. I've found that I look forward to my mornings of study, because it's peaceful and quiet, and I can watch the sun rise as I work. You might begin to look forward to morning homework sessions too! Maybe.

If not, that's fine! Reserve your work for the evenings. Just know, working in the evenings makes it so you have less time to do fun things. If you do it in the morning, you have more time later!

Now, I shall continue my rant on how to romanticize homework.

## How To Be Romantic

Imagine this: You're sitting at your desk, the lamp shining cheerily onto the books laying before you. You're listening to soft music, pen and highlighter in hand as you methodically take notes. You can smell the pages of the book, hear the click of the pen as you write, and the hum of your computer as it works in the background. It's early morning, or perhaps late at night. Your back is bathed in gentle sunlight as you work, and as your song grows in cadence, you glance out the window and smile contentedly.

Doesn't sound too bad, does it? Calm mornings, or calm evenings, working quietly.

Maybe it's even relaxing. Just like in the movies.

But how do you achieve that? What if it's the middle of the day?

Well, even if the daylight isn't that perfect blend of dawn and dusk, it's still possible to have a romanticized study session. Here are some basic steps that I follow every time I want to have a nice homework session:

1. Mood
2. Environment
3. Sustenance

I call it MESS. (I added the extra S because why not?)

**MOOD**: Set the mood! Dim/brighten the lights, wear the right clothes, put on your headphones.
**ENVIRONMENT**: Sit in your room, at a desk or on the bed. Go to a cafe. Chill in a library!
**SUSTENANCE**: Drink plenty of water with your coffee or tea, and get some food that fits the vibe, whether it be salad, pastries, or just an apple!

# Closing

All in all, this studying thing isn't so bad. The trick is to stop thinking so negatively of your abilities (easier said than done), and to completely romanticize EVERYTHING. Use *The Now Habit*, and practice MESS. I hope this (rather long) article helped you out!

You've got this.
